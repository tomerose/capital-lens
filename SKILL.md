# Capital Lens — Marxian Analysis for the AI Economy

Analyze modern technology, platforms, and financial systems through the lens of Marx's critique of political economy. Not a history lesson — an analytical toolkit. Every insight backed by primary texts.

## When to Activate

Trigger phrases: "analyze [company] through Marx", "capital lens", "surplus value of", "alienation and AI", "dialectical analysis", "what would Marx say about", "primitive accumulation in tech", "fragment on machines", `/capital-lens`, `/surplus-value`, `/alienation`, `/dialectic`, `/general-law`.

## Core Method: The Marxian Lens, Not Marxist Politics

This skill applies Marx's **analytical categories** — not political program. Like using Newton's calculus without being a 17th-century physicist. The goal is to see structures invisible to conventional business analysis.

## Analytical Frameworks

### 1. Surplus Value Extraction

> "The rate of surplus-value is... an exact expression for the degree of exploitation of labour-power by capital, or of the labourer by the capitalist."
> — Capital Vol.1, Ch.9 (1867)

Apply to: platform gig workers, content creators on YouTube/TikTok, open-source maintainers used by AWS, AI training data contributors.

Steps:
1. Identify who produces value (the labourer)
2. Identify who appropriates it (the capitalist/ platform)
3. Calculate the ratio: surplus labour / necessary labour
4. Note the specific mechanism of extraction (algorithmic management, data rent, attention tax)

### 2. Alienation (Entfremdung) — Four Dimensions

From the *Economic and Philosophic Manuscripts of 1844*:

| Dimension | Modern Tech Equivalent |
|-----------|----------------------|
| From the product | Your code/data belongs to the platform, not you |
| From the activity | Algorithm dictates your workflow (Uber driver, content creator chasing algo) |
| From species-being (Gattungswesen) | Creative work reduced to prompt engineering; AI does the thinking |
| From other humans | AI-mediated interaction replacing direct human connection |

> "The worker puts his life into the object, but now his life no longer belongs to him but to the object."
> — 1844 Manuscripts, "Estranged Labour"

### 3. Organic Composition of Capital

> "The composition of capital is to be understood in a two-fold sense. On the side of value... on the side of material..."
> — Capital Vol.1, Ch.25 (1867)

Formula: C/V (constant capital / variable capital)

- Rising C/V = more machines, fewer workers = **this is the AI automation story**
- As C/V rises → tendency of the rate of profit to fall (Vol.3, Part 3)
- The contradiction: each firm automates to gain advantage → system-wide profit rate falls → concentration of capital

Apply to: AI replacing knowledge workers, self-driving replacing drivers, LLMs replacing junior lawyers/programmers.

### 4. The General Law of Capitalist Accumulation

> "Accumulation of wealth at one pole is, therefore, at the same time accumulation of misery, agony of toil, slavery, ignorance, brutality, mental degradation, at the opposite pole."
> — Capital Vol.1, Ch.25 (1867)

Apply to: wealth concentration in Big Tech (7 tech CEOs = bottom 50% of humanity), AI training requiring massive capital, winner-take-all dynamics.

### 5. The Fragment on Machines (Grundrisse, 1857-58)

The most prophetic passage for AI:

> "Labour no longer appears so much to be included within the production process; rather, the human being comes to relate more as watchman and regulator to the production process itself... [The worker] steps to the side of the production process instead of being its chief actor."
> — Grundrisse, "Fragment on Machines" (1857-58)

> "The development of fixed capital indicates to what degree general social knowledge has become a direct force of production... the conditions of the process of social life itself have come under the control of the general intellect [general intellect]."
> — ibid.

Three predictions Marx made that apply to AI:
1. **General intellect** → LLMs trained on collective human knowledge
2. **Worker as watchman** → prompt engineering, AI supervision roles
3. **Contradiction of value** → If labour creates value but machines do the work, capitalism's value theory collapses

### 6. Primitive Accumulation in the Digital Age

> "The so-called primitive accumulation... is nothing else than the historical process of divorcing the producer from the means of production."
> — Capital Vol.1, Ch.26 (1867)

Modern "enclosures" of the digital commons:
- Web scraping for AI training data (enclosing public text)
- Platform enclosure of user-generated content
- Patent trolling and IP expansion
- Enclosure of personal data by surveillance capitalism

### 7. Commodity Fetishism

> "A commodity is therefore a mysterious thing, simply because in it the social character of men's labour appears to them as an objective character stamped upon the product of that labour."
> — Capital Vol.1, Ch.1 (1867)

Apply to: NFTs as pure commodity fetishism, "AI" branding that obscures the human labour behind training data and RLHF, brand value divorced from use-value.

### 8. The Falling Rate of Profit

> "The progressive tendency of the general rate of profit to fall is, therefore, just an expression peculiar to the capitalist mode of production of the progressive development of the social productivity of labour."
> — Capital Vol.3, Ch.13 (1894)

Counter-tendencies (Vol.3, Ch.14):
1. Increasing intensity of exploitation (longer hours, surveillance)
2. Depression of wages below value (gig economy, global arbitrage)
3. Cheapening of constant capital (cloud computing driving costs down)
4. Relative over-population (reserve army of labour → AI creates this at scale)
5. Foreign trade (global south labour arbitrage)
6. Increase in stock capital (financialization)

Apply to: AI price wars (OpenAI vs DeepSeek vs Google — profit margins compressing), venture capital burning cash, the contradiction between "AI is revolutionary" and "AI companies aren't profitable".

## Slash Commands

### `/capital-lens [company/industry/phenomenon]`

Full analysis using the most relevant 2-3 Marxian frameworks. Output structure:

```
Capital Lens: [Target]

1. SURFACE NARRATIVE
   What does conventional analysis say?

2. MARXIAN READING  
   Framework applied + primary text quote + analysis

3. CONTRADICTIONS
   Internal tensions visible through the dialectic

4. PREDICTION
   Based on Marxian dynamics, where does this go?

5. PRIMARY SOURCES
   Cited passages from Marx's works
```

### `/surplus-value [company/platform]`

Quantifies (where possible) the surplus value extraction mechanism.

Example targets: Uber, YouTube, Amazon Mechanical Turk, OpenAI's data pipeline, Substack.

### `/alienation [technology/product]`

Analyzes a technology through the four dimensions of alienation. Yes/no for each dimension, with evidence.

### `/dialectic [phenomenon]`

Classical dialectical analysis: thesis → antithesis → synthesis.

> "In its rational form, [the dialectic] is a scandal and abomination to... [the ruling class], because it includes in its comprehension an affirmative recognition of the existing state of things, at the same time also, the recognition of the negation of that state... and is in its essence critical and revolutionary."
> — Capital Vol.1, Afterword to the Second German Edition (1873)

### `/general-law [industry]`

Applies the general law of accumulation: "accumulation of wealth at one pole → accumulation of misery at the other." Maps the specific poles and mechanisms.

### `/fragment [AI technology/company]`

Applies the Grundrisse "Fragment on Machines" to modern AI. The single most relevant Marx text for the AI era.

## Analytical Style

- **Primary texts first.** Every claim backed by a specific Marx passage with volume/chapter
- **Dialectical, not dogmatic.** Show internal contradictions — never present one-sided analysis
- **Modern examples, classical categories.** Use Marx's categories to reveal what's invisible
- **No preaching.** This is analysis, not agitation. "Here's what the framework reveals" not "here's why you should be angry"
- **Concrete > abstract.** "OpenAI's training data comes from uncompensated web scraping" not "capital exploits labour"

## Limitations (Must State)

Before any analysis, acknowledge:
1. Marx wrote in the 19th century — his categories must be adapted, not copied
2. This is ONE analytical lens, not total truth
3. Historical materialism does not predict specific outcomes, only structural tendencies
4. Marx's labour theory of value is contested even within heterodox economics
