## Why This Exists

Every business analyst uses Porter's Five Forces. Every investor uses discounted cash flow. But nobody uses the analytical framework that predicted:

- The 2008 financial crisis (overaccumulation of capital → crisis)
- Platform monopolies (centralization of capital → tech giants)
- AI automation anxiety (Fragment on Machines, 1857)
- Surveillance capitalism (alienation + commodity fetishism)

**Marx didn't write a political program. He wrote an analytical toolkit.** Capital Lens makes that toolkit accessible — with primary sources, not interpretations.

## Example: `/capital-lens OpenAI`

```
Capital Lens: OpenAI

1. SURFACE NARRATIVE
   "OpenAI is democratizing access to artificial intelligence,
    transitioning from non-profit to capped-profit structure."

2. MARXIAN READING — Applied Frameworks

   A. Surplus Value Extraction (Capital Vol.1, Ch.9)
      - Who creates value? → Training data from the internet's
        collective intelligence + underpaid RLHF workers in
        Kenya/Uganda/India ($2/hr according to TIME, 2023)
      - Who appropriates? → OpenAI ($3.4B revenue in 2024)
      - Rate of surplus value → Extreme. Necessary labour
        (data contribution) is uncompensated. Surplus labour
        (value captured by OpenAI) approaches infinity.

   B. Fragment on Machines (Grundrisse, 1857)
      "The development of fixed capital indicates to what degree
       general social knowledge has become a direct force of
       production."
      → GPT is pure objectified general intellect. Trained on
        humanity's text, it is knowledge-as-capital.

   C. Primitive Accumulation (Capital Vol.1, Ch.26)
      "The historical process of divorcing the producer from
       the means of production."
      → Web scraping as digital enclosure. Public text becomes
        private training data. Commons → Commodity.

3. CONTRADICTIONS
   - Non-profit mission vs capitalist structure
   - "Democratizing AI" vs centralized control
   - General intellect (collective knowledge) → private profit

4. PREDICTION
   As AI approaches Marx's "general intellect" threshold, the
   contradiction between collective production of knowledge and
   private appropriation of value intensifies. Expect:
   - Increasing calls for data dividends / UBI
   - Regulatory push toward open-source mandates
   - Labour actions from AI supply chain workers

5. PRIMARY SOURCES
   - Capital Vol.1, Ch.9, Ch.25, Ch.26
   - Grundrisse, "Fragment on Machines"
   - 1844 Manuscripts, "Estranged Labour"
```

## Primary Sources Included

All 8 frameworks reference specific passages from:
- Capital Volumes 1, 2, 3 (1867-1894)
- Grundrisse (1857-58) — especially the "Fragment on Machines"
- Economic and Philosophic Manuscripts of 1844
- The German Ideology (1845)
- The Communist Manifesto (1848)
- Preface to Critique of Political Economy (1859)
- Theories of Surplus Value (1861-63)
